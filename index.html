My Story

29 businesses.
One calling.

How I spent years building businesses I was never meant to launch, and what the Lord taught me through every single one of them. This is not a success story in the way the world tells success stories. It is a story about obedience.

In this story

The skills I gathered The 29 businesses The word that changed things Where I am now

The beginning

The skills I gathered without knowing why

It did not start with a plan. It started with a stirring, a desire the Lord placed in me that I could not quite name yet. So I followed it.

I learned how to make graphics. Then video content. Then copywriting, and technical writing. I studied cybersecurity, what it means to protect a presence online, not just build one. I learned how to research, academically and otherwise. I learned how to use AI as a tool, not a crutch. I learned how to teach, how to walk people through a topic without losing them along the way.

I studied entrepreneurship. I learned lead generation. Not through one school or one course, but through real work, real jobs, real interviews that were forming something in me even when I could not see what yet. Some of those interviews I just knew the Lord was in them. They did not turn into jobs. They turned out to be classrooms.

"He stirred up my desires within me. The path did not look like a path. It looked like scattered pieces."

Graphic designCopywritingTechnical writingCybersecurityResearchAI toolsLead generationSocial media

The inventory

Twenty-nine businesses I was never meant to launch

At some point I did an inventory. I counted the businesses I had researched, branded, and positioned over the years. Twenty-nine. Each one with a name, an identity, a direction. Each one left on the shelf.

My first instinct was to see it as proof of capability. Look at what I can do. Maybe this next one is the one. Maybe now is the time.

"But I say again, the spirit of the Lord was not in that."

I tried anyway. And in the trying, something was off. Not failure. More like opening a door that swings smoothly but leads somewhere you were not supposed to go. Fine, but not right. When I pressed in, I kept hearing the same thing: He had not called me to have a business, nor to be a leader. He had called me to position myself as a leader's aid. Support is my strength.

The word

The word that made everything make sense

Once has the Lord spoken, but many times I have heard it. He was not calling me to the front. He was calling me to the person at the front, to stand beside them and make what they carry go further.

Every skill I had gathered suddenly had a context. The graphics were not for my brand. The cybersecurity was not for my systems. The copywriting, the research, the content strategy, all of it was equipment for someone else's journey.

"There were so many open doors to help others launch. I just kept walking past them looking for my own door."

The twenty-nine businesses were not wasted. They were a portfolio of everything I had learned about how to start something, and who I was finally meant to hand that knowledge to.

"Lean on, trust in, and be confident in the Lord with all your heart and mind and do not rely on your own insight or understanding."

Proverbs 3:5 AMPC

Now

An independent contractor. On purpose.

Today I work as an independent contractor, a consultant, not a company. When you work with me, you work with me directly. That is not a limitation. It is the point.

I work with Christian entrepreneurs internationally whose businesses are rooted in something bigger than market share, and who need their online presence to reflect that without becoming a performance. I bring design, copy, content, research, social perception, and cybersecurity. I bring years of paying attention. And I bring the understanding that being a faithful support to the right person is just as much a calling as being the one out front.

"The Lord was with me in all of it. Even the parts that did not go anywhere. Especially those."
